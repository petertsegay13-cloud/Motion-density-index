The Motion Density Index (MDi) transforms motion analysis by quantifying how intensity is concentrated within spatial frameworks.

MDi Theory

Formula:

MDi = (a × s) / d²
  • a = acceleration (m/s²)
  • s = speed (m/s)
  • d = distance (m)

Interpretation:

  • High MDi → rapid, accelerating motion over short distance
  • Low MDi → slower, dispersed movement across wider span
